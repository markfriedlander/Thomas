Privacy

Thomas does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data of any kind.

It has no accounts, no analytics, no advertising identifiers. Your data is your data. There are no servers operated by the developer.

Your photographs. Both machines run on your iPhone: the vision model that describes what it sees, and the drawing model that redraws the scene from those words. Thomas does not upload your photographs to the developer or to anyone else. Your photographs are saved in your phone's photos library.

Apple Intelligence. When the eye is Apple Intelligence, inference is on-device when offline; when connected, Apple's Private Cloud Compute may be used (encrypted in transit, processed in non-persistent memory on Apple-controlled servers). Choosing a downloaded local model keeps everything on your device, with no network calls at all.

Location. Thomas stamps the place on the frame, the way a camera has always stamped the date. Stated plainly: to turn your coordinates into a place name, the coordinate is sent to Apple's mapping service, which is how reverse geocoding works on iOS. Thomas does not store your location, does not attach it to your photographs as data, and does not send it anywhere else. If you decline location, no lookup is ever made and frames simply carry no place stamp.

Your photo library. Saving a finished frame uses add-only permission, which lets Thomas add pictures without being able to read the ones already there. If you choose to feed an existing picture into the camera, the system picker hands Thomas that one picture and nothing else.

Model downloads. Models are fetched from Hugging Face over standard HTTPS, only at your explicit request. Once downloaded they run entirely on your device. They are shared with the developer's other apps on the same phone, so a model you already have is never downloaded twice.

Thomas is open source. If you would rather read the code than take any of this on faith, it is at github.com/markfriedlander/Thomas.
